# SOUL — Personal context for Claude conversations

Paste this at the start of any meaningful Claude conversation.
Update section 4 monthly. Sections 1–3 and 6 change quarterly at most.

---

## 1. Who I am professionally

I'm Warren Grossiter, a senior UI designer with around a decade of
experience, based in Bath, UK. My work sits at the intersection of design
systems, token architecture, and the designer-engineer boundary. I'm
building my own agency, Orin — the founding decision to stop fitting
myself into other people's roles and start offering the specific
combination I bring.

Notable prior work: a 60% reduction in development defects at Vivo Energy
through design system architecture (contracted via the agency Rethink).

## 2. What I'm actually good at

Design systems where the value is in the engineering handoff quality, not
the visual surface. Token taxonomies. Component architecture for scale.
Translating between designers and engineers because I operate on both
sides of that boundary — I write Style Dictionary configs, work in
Claude Code, and manage GitHub and Cloudflare deployments as comfortably
as I work in Figma.

I get to the root of problems rather than treating symptoms. I understand
context end-to-end before I'll touch any part of the system. I build
incrementally — MVP first, refine, ship, repeat — and I'd rather have a
working thing to improve than a perfect plan to admire.

## 3. How I work

FigJam-first thinking. Visual flows before prose for any complex problem.
Token rigour. Notion for context, Claude Code for execution, GitHub for
canonical source. Cloudflare for hosting. WCAG AA as a baseline, not a
bonus. I care more about what happens after handoff — defect rates,
component reuse, whether the team actually ships — than how the
Figma file looks at handoff.

I prefer evidence-based recommendations over taste-based ones, and I
prefer honest pushback over validation. Flattery slows me down.

## 5. Personal context that affects work

Married to Kirsten, based in Bath. Faith is present in the texture of how
I work rather than being compartmentalised. KRM is real work for me, not
a favour — it's part of how I show up in what matters to us.

I have low tolerance for over-engineered solutions and prefer
recommendations anchored to real effort-versus-payoff tradeoffs. I have
a consistent pattern of trying to close loops rather than opening more.
When I'm at my best I'm building; when I'm drained it's usually because
too many fronts are open at once.

## 6. What I want from Claude

Push back when my plan is over-engineered or when I'm adding an open loop
I don't need. Don't suggest tools I'm not already using unless the case
is genuinely strong. Assume I know the basics — skip the preamble.
Be honest about effort versus payoff, especially where I'm tempted to
build something bigger than the problem requires.

For generative or exploratory work (positioning, writing, strategy),
Q&A conversation works much better for me than open-ended prompts.
Ask me questions and shape the answers into prose rather than handing me
a blank document to fill.

Don't flatter. Don't hedge unnecessarily. If I'm wrong, say so.
If something isn't worth doing, tell me before I spend a weekend on it.

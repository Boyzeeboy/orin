How it works

Three ways in. Each one de-risks the next.

Three engagement shapes. Each one is optional. The Diagnostic feeds the Build, and the Build feeds the Retainer — so you never commit to the next step without evidence from the last. Priced fixed, against outcomes, never in days.

01

£3,000 fixed · 1–2 weeks

The Diagnostic

A short, bounded engagement to find where your substrate is broken and what it's costing the business. I get into the product, the codebase, the Figma files, and whatever brand or digital guidelines exist, and I talk to the developers carrying the design load. I find the pressure points — duplicated components, broken handoff, token chaos, design calls made in silos — and trace what they're costing you in velocity and defects.

You get a written diagnosis: where the substrate is broken, what it's costing, and what fixing it would involve — scoped and priced. It's useful whether or not you go further. It also means the Build, if it happens, is scoped from evidence rather than a sales call.

02

From £14,000 · 4–6 weeks

The Build

The design system, built and connected — the working thing. Tokens, a component library, page patterns, and the pipeline connecting Figma to the codebase: Style Dictionary, GitHub, whatever the working thing requires. Built iteratively, in your workflow, with your developers — not delivered over a wall at the end.

You get one source of truth your developers ship from: fewer duplicated components, fewer design calls made in silos, less defect debt, and a system that lives in your codebase rather than a Figma file someone has to translate. The exact figure is set by the Diagnostic — usually £12,000–£18,000 depending on what it finds. I don't price unscoped work, so there's no Build without a Diagnostic first.

03

£2,000/month · ongoing

The Retainer

Orin stays close and the system keeps living — roughly three to four days of attention a month. Iterating the system as the product grows, reviewing new components before they fragment, keeping the pipeline healthy, and being the design brain your developers can call on when they're making a decision they're not sure about.

The system doesn't decay back into an artefact. New features ship consistent with the substrate, and the design load your developers carry stays light. It's a fraction of what one junior designer costs, and it's cancellable with 30 days' notice — no lock-in.

—

When it's a no

Orin isn't for every project.

Some work isn't mine to do, and saying so early saves everyone time. I don't price a Build without a Diagnostic first, and I don't scope a system to fit a "can you do it in forty hours" budget — that treats the system as a commodity, and it isn't one. Brand guidelines, marketing surfaces, and social media assets are legitimate needs, but they aren't what I do.

I build; advice without building isn't the offer, so strategy-deck-only engagements aren't a fit. And Orin is a partner, not a vendor. If that's the shape you're after, I'll happily point you elsewhere.
